Research Synergy

Aligning Commercial Innovation with Foundational Academic Research

The Hodge Research Group & Power System Security Assessment

Our work is deeply aligned with the cutting-edge research conducted by Professor Bri-Mathias Hodge's group at CU Boulder and NREL. A key focus of their work, led by PhD student Muhy Eddin Za'ter, is accelerating Power System Security Assessment (PSSA) using advanced AI, specifically **Multi-Task Learning (MTL)** frameworks.

The Perfect Complement: Classification vs. Regression

Analysis of their research reveals a powerful synergy. Their work and our GridOS project solve two different, but equally critical, sequential questions an operator faces after a grid fault:

Hodge Group's Research (Classification)

Their MTL framework answers the first question: **"After the fault, will the system remain stable?"** It classifies the post-contingency state into one of several stability categories (e.g., stable, voltage unstable, transiently unstable).

GridOS GNN (Regression)

Our GNN answers the essential follow-up question: **"Given the system *is* stable, where are the new high-stress points and what is the exact loading on every line?"** It provides a high-resolution, quantitative prediction.

This positions GridOS as the perfect "next step" in a complete security assessment workflow, providing the specific, actionable detail needed for an operator to take corrective action.
