The Economics Engine

High-Fidelity Market Forecasting with Transformer Models

Challenge: Predicting Volatility in the NYISO Market

The N.Y.C. load zone (Zone J) is a classic "load pocket" with structural transmission constraints and a reliance on expensive in-city peaker plants. This creates a highly volatile market where accurately forecasting Locational Marginal Prices (LMPs) is critical for managing financial risk and capturing opportunity.

Methodology: Transformer Architecture vs. Industry Baselines

To prove our capability, we benchmarked a state-of-the-art **Transformer model** against two standards: a statistical **SARIMA** model and a powerful machine learning competitor, **XGBoost**. The Transformer's self-attention mechanism is uniquely suited to capture the complex, long-range dependencies in energy market data that linear models miss.

Data Pipeline: Real-World NYISO Operational Data

The model was trained and tested on a rich dataset synthesized from real NYISO reports for September 30, 2025, including:

  • Day-Ahead Market (DAM) & Hour-Ahead Market (HAM) Energy Reports
  • Real-Time Generation Fuel Mix
  • Zonal Day-Ahead LMPs for N.Y.C.

Results: A Decisive Performance Advantage

The GridOS Transformer model demonstrated a significant and commercially meaningful improvement in forecasting accuracy over all baselines.
